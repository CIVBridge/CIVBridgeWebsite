Praxis I Design Summary: The Splartz

The Splartz is a dynamic air pump platform created to address the common issue of chair wobble in university study environments. Designed for square-legged chairs, it automatically adjusts to differences in leg height using internal elastic air pockets, thereby enhancing user comfort and focus without requiring permanent installation. The platform can support up to 150 kg and correct unevenness ranging from 1.25 mm to 12 mm, achieving a 98% correction rate during testing. Its compact size and adaptability allow for easy deployment across campus study spaces, promoting stability, user safety, and a more inclusive seating experience for all students.

CTMF 1: Framing Requirements with NSPIRE

Integrating the NSPIRE safety standard (Figure 1) into our project requirements enhanced my understanding of the engineering design process by emphasizing the importance of user safety and functionality. NSPIRE requires that designs minimize sharp edges and puncture risks to protect users, particularly in public spaces. We used this as a basis to frame a requirement against sharp corners or exposed mechanical elements. This led to design solutions with rounded profiles, soft enclosures, and protective casings. The NSPIRE framework became a functional constraint and a design guide that helped us iterate on safer, more user-aware alternatives.
